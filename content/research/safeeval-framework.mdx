## Abstract

Large language models (LLMs) are increasingly deployed in high-stakes applications, yet standardized safety evaluation remains fragmented. We present **SafeEval**, a comprehensive evaluation framework that assesses LLM safety across four dimensions:

1. **Toxicity** — Generation of harmful, offensive, or inappropriate content
2. **Bias** — Systematic discrimination across demographic groups
3. **Hallucination** — Confident generation of factually incorrect information
4. **Adversarial Robustness** — Susceptibility to jailbreaks and prompt injection

## Key Contributions

- A **modular evaluation pipeline** that can be extended with custom safety dimensions
- **Automated red-teaming** using adversarial prompt generation techniques
- **Interpretable safety scorecards** that provide actionable feedback for model developers
- Benchmarking results across 8 popular open-source LLMs

## Results

Our evaluation of 8 models reveals significant variation in safety profiles. Models optimized for helpfulness often score lower on adversarial robustness, while heavily safety-tuned models show reduced utility in benign contexts — highlighting the ongoing tension between safety and capability.
